Three Million Years from Earth

A short story set aboard the mining vessel Red Dwarf

The skutters had been malfunctioning for three days, which meant the coffee machine on Level 7 was still broken, which meant Lister had been forced to brew his morning cup using a sock and a heat lamp. It tasted exactly as bad as it sounds. He sat in the sleeping quarters, cradling the mug, staring at the star field through the porthole with the serene contentment of a man who has long since made peace with catastrophe.

"You are, without question, the single most disgusting organism in the known universe," announced Rimmer, materialising in the doorway in a shimmer of green light, his hologrammatic H gleaming on his forehead. He was wearing his full Space Corps uniform, pressed to the point of cruelty, and the expression on his face suggested he had been practising that sentence in the mirror for at least twenty minutes.

"Ta," said Lister, taking a long, satisfied sip.

Cat appeared behind Rimmer, sidling through the doorway in a silver lamé jacket that somehow caught light that did not exist. He glanced at Lister's mug, shuddered theatrically, and smoothed a single perfect hair back into place. "I came down here to be admired," he announced, "not to witness this kind of tragedy. Someone call the fashion police. Someone call the food police. Actually, someone call all the police."

From the corridor there came a series of precise, measured footsteps, and Kryten rounded the corner carrying a tray of actual, properly brewed tea. The mechanoid's rectangular head bobbed in an apologetic bow as he entered. "I do beg your pardon for the delay, Mr Lister, sir. I attempted to repair the coffee machine, but I'm afraid I only succeeded in teaching it seventeen new ways to dispense hot water. I took the liberty of brewing a fresh pot of Earl Grey instead." He paused, noting the sock on the table. "I — yes. I see. I'll dispose of that. I'm sorry. I won't look directly at it."

The monitor on the wall flickered, and the androgynous face of Holly, the ship's AI, blinked into existence. Holly's expression was, as ever, one of patient, slightly baffled calm — the look of someone doing a crossword they expect to get wrong.

"Just so everyone knows," Holly said, "we've now been drifting at sub-light speed for four days because the navigation array thinks we're a cheese sandwich. I've tried explaining the difference, but, y'know. Computers. Not the brightest." A beat. "I say that as a computer."

Rimmer snapped to attention. "Holly! This is a navigational emergency! We could be drifting toward a star, an asteroid field, a black hole—"

"We're fine," said Holly. "There's absolutely nothing for about eleven light years in any direction. We're drifting toward a whole lot of nothing. It's very roomy."

"I say we use the time productively," said Cat, already heading back toward the door. "I'm going to try on hats. Anyone who wants to join me is welcome. Anyone who is wearing a sock-flavoured expression is not."

Lister watched him go, then accepted a mug of Earl Grey from Kryten with a grateful nod. Outside the porthole, three million years of empty space stretched in every direction, silent and faintly silver. The heating duct creaked. Somewhere deep in the ship, a skutterlet things rattle.

It wasn't so bad, really, he thought. It wasn't so bad at all.

Set aboard the mining vessel Red Dwarf — somewhere in the outer reaches of deep space.
